Background

Volume conversion is essential in cooking, chemistry, engineering, and fuel measurement. The litre is standard metric; gallons, cups, and fl oz are US/imperial. US and imperial gallons differ by 20%.

US vs Imperial Gallons

US gallon = 3.785 L; imperial = 4.546 L — a 20% difference. Australia uses litres.

Cups and Cooking

US cup = 236.6 ml; Australian metric cup = 250 ml. Small differences matter in baking.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you convert litres to gallons?

Divide by 3.785 (US) or 4.546 (imperial). 10 L = 2.64 US gal or 2.20 imp gal.

How many ml in a cup?

US cup = 236.6 ml; Australian metric cup = 250 ml.

US vs imperial gallon?

US: 3.785 L (128 fl oz). Imperial: 4.546 L (160 fl oz). Imperial is ~20% larger.
