Background

Speed conversion is essential when travelling internationally, or working in aviation/maritime. Metric uses km/h, US uses mph, aviation uses knots, science uses m/s.

Why Aviation Uses Knots

A knot = 1 nautical mile/hour. Nautical miles align with Earth's coordinates (1 NM = 1 minute of latitude). Airspeed and maritime speed are measured in knots worldwide.

Speed vs Velocity

Speed is magnitude only; velocity includes direction. For conversions, we deal with speed. 1 m/s = 3.6 km/h.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you convert km/h to mph?

Divide by 1.60934. 100 km/h = 62.14 mph. Quick: ×0.6.

How fast is a knot in km/h?

1 knot = 1.852 km/h. 30 knots = 55.56 km/h.

What is 1 m/s in km/h?

1 m/s = 3.6 km/h. Multiply by 3.6.
