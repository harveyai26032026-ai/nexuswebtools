Background

Speed conversion is essential when travelling internationally, working in aviation or maritime industries, or comparing vehicle performance across regions. The metric system uses kilometres per hour (km/h), the US uses miles per hour (mph), aviation and maritime use knots (nautical miles per hour), and science uses metres per second (m/s).

Why Aviation Uses Knots

A knot is one nautical mile per hour. Nautical miles are based on the Earth's circumference (1 nautical mile = 1 minute of latitude), making navigation calculations straightforward. This is why airspeed, wind speed, and maritime speed are all measured in knots worldwide, regardless of the country's general measurement system.

Applications

Driving Abroad

Convert speed limits when driving in a country with a different system. 100 km/h = 62 mph. 70 mph = 113 km/h. Quick conversion helps you stay legal and safe.

Aviation and Sailing

Airspeed indicators read in knots. Converting to km/h or mph helps non-pilots understand aircraft speeds. A typical cruising speed of 450 knots equals approximately 833 km/h or 518 mph.

Sports and Fitness

Running pace and cycling speed are often measured in km/h or mph depending on the country. Convert between them to compare performance with international benchmarks.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you convert km/h to mph?

Divide km/h by 1.60934. For example, 100 km/h ÷ 1.60934 = 62.14 mph. A quick approximation: multiply by 0.6 — 100 km/h ≈ 60 mph.

How fast is a knot in km/h?

1 knot = 1.852 km/h. So 30 knots = 55.56 km/h. Knots are used in aviation and maritime contexts because they are based on nautical miles, which align with the Earth's coordinate system.

What is 1 m/s in km/h?

1 m/s = 3.6 km/h. To convert, multiply by 3.6. For example, 10 m/s = 36 km/h. This is useful for converting scientific measurements to everyday units.
