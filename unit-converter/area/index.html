Background

Area conversion is crucial in real estate, agriculture, construction, and land management. Different countries and industries use different area units: square metres and hectares in metric countries, square feet and acres in imperial countries. Australia uses a mix — square metres for buildings and hectares for rural land, with acres still used colloquially in farming.

Hectares vs Acres

A hectare (10,000 m²) is the metric standard for land area. An acre (4,047 m²) is the imperial equivalent. 1 hectare = 2.471 acres. Australian rural properties are typically measured in hectares, but US and UK properties may use acres.

Applications

Real Estate

Property listings may use square metres (Australia, Europe) or square feet (US, Hong Kong). Convert between them to understand property sizes when comparing international listings.

Agriculture and Farming

Farm sizes are measured in hectares (Australia, Europe) or acres (US, UK). Converting between them is essential for international agricultural comparisons and commodity calculations.

Construction and Renovation

Floor plans, tile quantities, and paint coverage are all calculated by area. Convert between units when working with international suppliers or specifications.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you convert square metres to square feet?

Multiply square metres by 10.7639. For example, 100 m² × 10.7639 = 1,076.39 sq ft. A quick approximation: multiply by 10.8 or simply multiply by 11 for a rough estimate.

How many acres are in a hectare?

1 hectare = 2.47105 acres. 1 acre = 0.404686 hectares. For a quick approximation, 1 hectare is roughly 2.5 acres.

How do you convert square feet to square metres?

Divide square feet by 10.7639 (or multiply by 0.092903). For example, 1,000 sq ft ÷ 10.7639 = 92.9 m². This is a common conversion for comparing apartment sizes.
