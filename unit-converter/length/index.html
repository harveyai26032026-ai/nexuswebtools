Background

Length measurement is one of the oldest and most fundamental types of quantification. The metric system is the international standard; the imperial system remains common in the US.

Metric vs Imperial Systems

The metric system is decimal-based — each unit is 10, 100, or 1000 times the next. Imperial units have irregular ratios (12 inches per foot, 3 feet per yard), making manual conversion error-prone.

The 1959 International Agreement

In 1959, English-speaking nations standardised the inch at exactly 25.4 mm. Before this, the US and British inches differed slightly.

Applications

Construction and Building

Building plans may use metric or imperial measurements. Converting between systems is essential for international projects and US-sourced specifications.

Manufacturing and Engineering

Precision engineering requires converting between metric and imperial tolerances. ±0.005 inches = ±0.127 mm — accuracy that matters in machining.

Travel and Navigation

Converting between km and miles is essential when driving abroad. 100 km/h ≈ 62 mph.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you convert inches to millimetres?

Multiply inches by 25.4. For example, 5 in × 25.4 = 127 mm. This is exact.

How do you convert metres to feet?

Multiply metres by 3.28084. For example, 2 m = 6.56 ft.

How do you convert km to miles?

Multiply km by 0.621371. For example, 100 km = 62.14 miles.
