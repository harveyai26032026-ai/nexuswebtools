Background

Length measurement is one of the oldest and most fundamental types of quantification. The metric system (millimetres, centimetres, metres, kilometres) is the international standard, used in science, engineering, and everyday life in most countries. The imperial system (inches, feet, yards, miles) remains common in the United States, and in certain industries worldwide such as construction and aviation.

Metric vs Imperial Systems

The metric system is decimal-based — each unit is 10, 100, or 1000 times the next. This makes conversions within metric straightforward. Imperial units have irregular ratios (12 inches per foot, 3 feet per yard, 1760 yards per mile), making manual conversion error-prone. Our converter eliminates these calculation errors.

The 1959 International Agreement

In 1959, English-speaking nations agreed to standardise the inch at exactly 25.4 millimetres. Before this, the US inch and British inch differed slightly, causing precision engineering issues. This agreement is why all modern length conversions are exact and consistent worldwide.

Applications

Length conversions are used across many fields and everyday situations:

Construction and Building

Building plans may use metric (Australia, Europe) or imperial (US) measurements. Converting between systems is essential for international projects, importing materials, or working with US-sourced plans and specifications.

Manufacturing and Engineering

Precision engineering often requires converting between metric and imperial tolerances. A specification of ±0.005 inches equals ±0.127 mm — accuracy that matters in machining and quality control.

Travel and Navigation

Converting between kilometres and miles is essential when driving in a country that uses a different system. Speed limits, distances, and fuel efficiency all require conversion.

DIY and Home Improvement

Furniture dimensions, fabric measurements, and room sizes may be listed in either system. Quick conversion ensures you buy the right materials and fit items correctly.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you convert inches to millimetres?

Multiply inches by 25.4. For example, 5 inches × 25.4 = 127 mm. This is an exact conversion based on the 1959 international agreement that defined 1 inch = 25.4 mm exactly.

How do you convert metres to feet?

Multiply metres by 3.28084. For example, 2 metres × 3.28084 = 6.56168 feet. To get feet and inches: the whole number is feet (6), multiply the decimal by 12 for inches (0.56168 × 12 = 6.74 inches), so 2 m = 6 ft 6.7 in.

How do you convert kilometres to miles?

Multiply kilometres by 0.621371. For example, 100 km × 0.621371 = 62.1371 miles. A quick approximation: multiply by 0.6 and add 3.5% — 100 km ≈ 63 miles (close enough for mental maths).

Why does the US still use imperial units?

Historical momentum. The US adopted imperial measurements before the metric system existed and never mandated a full transition. The cost of converting road signs, manufacturing standards, and public infrastructure is estimated in the hundreds of billions of dollars.
