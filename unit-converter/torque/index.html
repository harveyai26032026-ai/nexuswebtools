Background

Torque measures rotational force. Nm is SI; ft-lbs is US automotive. Correct torque is critical for bolt tightening and engine specifications.

Torque in Automotive

Passenger car: 150–300 Nm. Heavy truck: 500–2,500 Nm. Always specified alongside RPM.

Bolt Torque Specs

M10 bolt: 40–50 Nm (30–37 ft-lbs). Over-tightening breaks bolts; under-tightening causes failures.

Applications

Automotive Maintenance

Wheel nuts: 100–120 Nm (74–89 ft-lbs). Wrong values cause wheel failure.

Mechanical Engineering

Motor/gearbox specs in Nm or ft-lbs. Convert for international components.

Bicycle Maintenance

Carbon components: 5–8 Nm. Over-tightening damages expensive parts.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you convert Nm to ft-lbs?

Divide by 1.3558. 100 Nm = 73.76 ft-lbs.

How do you convert ft-lbs to Nm?

Multiply by 1.3558. 80 ft-lbs = 108.46 Nm.

Torque vs power?

Torque = rotational force (Nm). Power = torque × angular velocity. 300 Nm at 4,000 RPM ≈ 125.7 kW.
