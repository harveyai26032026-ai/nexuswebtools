Background

Force is fundamental. Newton (SI): force to accelerate 1 kg at 1 m/s². Pound-force (US engineering). kgf (non-SI, still used). 1 kg mass exerts ~9.81 N on Earth.

Mass vs Force

1 kg mass exerts 9.81 N force due to gravity = 1 kgf. In space, same mass = zero force.

N and lbf in Engineering

US specs use lbf; international uses N. 1 lbf = 4.448 N.

Applications

Structural Engineering

Office floor: 2–3 kN/m² (40–60 lbf/ft²). International projects need both units.

Materials Testing

Tensile strength: MPa (N/mm²) or psi (lbf/in²). Convert for international material comparisons.

Aerospace and Automotive

Aerodynamic drag: 300–500 N at highway speed. Convert to lbf for US specs.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you convert N to lbf?

Divide by 4.448. 100 N = 22.48 lbf.

kg vs kgf?

kg = mass; kgf = force. 1 kgf = 9.80665 N (gravity on 1 kg). Distinct in physics/engineering.

How many N in 1 lbf?

1 lbf = 4.44822 N (exact, based on standard gravity).
