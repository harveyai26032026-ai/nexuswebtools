Background

Power is the rate of energy transfer. Watts (SI), kilowatts (electricity), horsepower (engines), BTU/hr (HVAC). Converting is essential for comparing engines and sizing equipment.

Watt, Kilowatt, Megawatt

1 kW = 1,000 W. 1 MW = 1,000,000 W. Household: 1–3 kW avg. Car: 75–150 kW. Power station: hundreds of MW.

Mechanical vs Electrical HP

Mechanical HP = 745.7 W. Metric HP (PS) = 735.5 W. Boiler HP = 9,810 W.

Applications

Automotive

200 HP = 149 kW. EU specs use kW; US uses HP.

Electrical Appliances

Kettle: ~2 kW. Heater: ~2.4 kW. LED bulb: ~10 W. Converting estimates electricity costs.

Solar Energy

Residential panel: 300–400 W. System sizes in kW (e.g. 6.6 kW).

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you convert HP to kW?

Multiply mechanical HP by 0.7457. 150 HP = 111.86 kW. Metric HP: ×0.7355.

How many watts does a household use?

Average: 1.5–2.5 kW continuous, 15–20 kWh/day. Peak: 5–8 kW.

What is BTU/hr in watts?

Divide by 3.412. 10,000 BTU/hr ≈ 2,931 W ≈ 2.9 kW.
