Background

Viscosity = resistance to flow. Dynamic: Pa·s (SI) or cP (industry). Kinematic: m²/s or cSt. Essential for lubrication and chemical engineering.

Dynamic vs Kinematic

Dynamic (Pa·s, cP) = internal resistance. Kinematic (m²/s, cSt) = dynamic ÷ density. Engine oil: 100–200 cP at room temp.

Common Viscosities

Water: ~1 cP. Honey: 2,000–10,000 cP. Motor oil: 50–1,000 cP depending on grade/temperature.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you convert cP to Pa·s?

Divide by 1,000. 100 cP = 0.1 Pa·s. 1 cP = 0.001 Pa·s = 1 mPa·s.

Viscosity of water?

At 20°C: ~1.002 cP (dynamic) or ~1.004 cSt (kinematic).

cP vs cSt?

cP = dynamic viscosity. cSt = kinematic viscosity. cSt = cP ÷ density (g/cm³).
