Background

Understanding emergency fund is essential for Australians managing their finances, especially with fluctuating interest rates.

Why Emergency Fund matters

In the Australian context, emergency fund can affect savings, loan repayments, and retirement planning, influencing long‑term wealth.

Key factors

Interest rate, compounding frequency, and term length are crucial when calculating emergency fund outcomes.

Applications

How much should I save for unexpected expenses?

Consider saving 3-6 months' worth of living expenses in an easily accessible savings account, such as a high-yield savings account or a money market fund. This will provide a cushion for unexpected expenses, such as car repairs or medical bills, without sacrificing your long-term financial goals.

I'm a student with irregular income from part-time jobs

As a student, you may have irregular income from part-time jobs. To use the emergency fund calculator, enter your total annual income from these jobs in the 'Other Income' field. The calculator will then adjust the results based on your income level and expenses.

Using the Emergency Fund Calculator for Irregular Income

If you have irregular income, such as self-employment income or income from a side hustle, you can use the emergency fund calculator to determine how much you need in your emergency fund. Simply enter your total income and the calculator will adjust the results accordingly.
