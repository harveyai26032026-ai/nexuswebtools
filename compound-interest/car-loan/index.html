Background

Car loans typically use fixed or variable interest rates that compound daily or monthly. The total interest paid depends on the rate, loan term, and whether a balloon payment is involved. A car is a depreciating asset — it loses value from the moment you drive it off the lot — so minimising loan interest is essential to avoid being underwater (owing more than the car is worth).

Balloon Payments and Compounding

A balloon payment reduces monthly repayments by deferring a lump sum to the end of the loan. While this lowers the regular payment, you pay more total interest because a larger principal is compounding for longer. On a $30,000 car loan at 7% over 5 years, a $10,000 balloon adds approximately $1,400 in extra interest.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good interest rate for a car loan?

As of 2025, good car loan rates start from approximately 5–6% for new cars and 6–8% for used cars. Dealer finance is often 2–4% higher than bank or credit union rates. Always compare the comparison rate, not just the advertised rate.

Is a balloon payment worth it on a car loan?

A balloon payment lowers your monthly repayments but increases total interest paid and leaves you with a large payment at the end. It can be useful if you plan to trade in the car before the loan ends, but otherwise you pay more in total.
