About Concrete Calculator

Concrete Calculator is a free, client-side web tool that instantly estimates concrete volume, bag counts, mix ratios, and saw-cut spacing. It runs entirely in your browser — no sign-up, no app to install, and no data is sent to our servers.

Who we are

Concrete Calculator is operated by Nexus Web Tools, an Australian-based digital tools publisher that builds practical, browser-based calculators and utilities for construction, engineering, and finance professionals. Our team has over a decade of combined experience in construction estimating, civil engineering, and web application development. We created Concrete Calculator after repeatedly seeing DIY builders and tradespeople order the wrong amount of concrete — either too little (leaving a half-poured slab) or too much (wasting hundreds of dollars). The tool was designed to be fast, accurate, and easy enough for anyone to use on a phone at the building site.

What we offer

The calculator supports both metric and imperial units and includes:

  • Volume estimation for slabs, footings, and round columns — with automatic unit conversion.
  • Bag count estimates for 20 kg, 30 kg, 40 kg, 60 lb and 80 lb bags.
  • Mix design guidance — cement, sand, and aggregate quantities by strength grade (20–40 MPa).
  • Saw-cut (control joint) spacing recommendations based on industry rules of thumb.
  • Graded-land mode for calculating concrete volume on uneven or sloping ground using four corner depths.
  • Volume subtraction for slabs with cut-outs, footings, or voids.

Our approach to accuracy

All calculations use established engineering formulas and industry-standard mix ratios published by the Cement Concrete & Aggregates Association (CCAA) and the American Concrete Institute (ACI). The waste allowance, bag yield figures, and joint spacing rules reflect real-world supplier data and construction practice — not theoretical assumptions. That said, the tool is an estimator, not a substitute for professional engineering advice. Always confirm structural requirements with a qualified engineer or your concrete supplier.
